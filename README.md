# Chest Pain Navigator

Chest Pain Navigator is a clinical decision support tool designed to assist clinicians in the evaluation of patients presenting with chest pain using structured guideline-based pathways.

The tool guides the user through a series of clinical questions and automatically generates recommendations based on commonly accepted cardiology decision algorithms.

---

## Overview

Chest pain is one of the most common reasons for emergency department visits and cardiology consultations. Determining the appropriate diagnostic strategy can be complex and depends on multiple factors including patient age, sex, clinical presentation, and known coronary artery disease.

Chest Pain Navigator simplifies this process by translating clinical decision pathways into an interactive tool.

---

## Features

- Structured **clinical decision pathway**
- Automatic navigation through the algorithm
- Risk stratification based on **age and sex**
- Differentiation between **stable and unstable chest pain**
- Evaluation of **acute vs non-acute presentations**
- Branching pathways for **known CAD vs no CAD**
- Clear display of **Class I and Class IIa recommendations**
- Clean and intuitive clinician-focused interface

---

## Clinical Logic

The tool integrates several key elements used in chest pain evaluation:

- Age and sex-based risk stratification
- Detection of unstable features requiring urgent management
- Differentiation between stable and acute chest pain
- Evaluation of patients with known coronary artery disease
- Selection of appropriate diagnostic strategies including:

  - Coronary CT Angiography (CCTA)
  - Stress testing (CMR, PET, SPECT, Stress Echocardiography)
  - Exercise ECG
  - Invasive coronary angiography

## Disclaimer

This tool was created to **assist clinicians in clinical decision making**.

It is **not intended to replace clinical judgment**, and all medical decisions should be made within the context of a full clinical evaluation.
